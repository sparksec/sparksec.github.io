Dipping my toes into Offensive Cyber Security

Part 1 (of probably many ramblings)

OK so it goes by many names I called it offensive cyber security, it could be called ethical hacking, penetration testing, web app hacking, white hat hacking, alright Iâ€™ll stop there you get the picture.

Whatever you might call it in a nutshell it means getting paid to find flaws, bugs, vulnerabilities in any service, app either running on a network, server, client, or website and then exploiting that to gain access to something you are not supposed to and best of all it is legal. Just remember #hackingsnotacrime as long as you have permission to engage the target.

Now just a quick overview of the hat color I mentioned above, White Hat Hacker is a hacker who stays in the legal boundaries of the task he has been given. A Black Hat Hacker is the polar opposite and will hack anything the usual motivation being money, sometimes political agenda and probably sometimes just to see if they can.

Now there is a middle ground, called a Grey Hat Hacker, best way to explain this is the grey hat hacks illegally but for the morally right reasons, for instance trying to bring criminals to justice, or a popular one on Youtube is hacking into Indian scam call centers and causing havoc for them. Both being technically illegal but you would probably escape any punishment for them. small caveat to that last statement I am not an expert!

So enough rambling for now I will see you in the next part.
